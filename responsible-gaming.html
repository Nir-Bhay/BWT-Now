Budget

Set a Budget Before You Start

One of the most effective responsible-gaming habits is setting a budget before you log in.

Decide the maximum amount you are comfortable spending for entertainment. This amount should come only from spare money, not from funds needed for rent, food, bills, school fees, family support, debt repayment, medical costs, or emergencies.

Once your budget is finished, stop. Do not add more money because you feel close to recovering a loss or because a match still has overs remaining.

A Simple Budget Rule

Before using Reddy Book Club, ask yourself:

“If I lose this amount today, will my normal life still be okay tomorrow?”

If the answer is no, do not deposit or play.

Budgeting is not only for beginners. Expert bettors also need limits because confidence can sometimes lead to bigger mistakes.

Time limits

Set Time Limits, Not Just Money Limits

Responsible gaming is not only about money. Time also matters.

Long sessions can lead to poor decisions, especially during live cricket, football matches, or fast casino games. The longer you play, the easier it becomes to react emotionally instead of thinking clearly.

Set a time limit before starting. For example, decide that you will check your account for 20 minutes, follow one match, or play for a short fixed session. When the time is over, stop and take a break.

Warning signs that you are spending too much time include:

  • Checking markets again and again
  • Watching games only because you placed money
  • Staying awake late to follow results
  • Ignoring work, study, or family time
  • Feeling restless when you are not logged in
  • Playing longer than planned

Time control helps protect your focus, sleep, and daily life.

Loss recovery

Never Chase Losses

Chasing losses means increasing your activity after losing because you want to recover quickly. This is one of the most common risky behaviours in sports betting and casino gaming.

A loss can feel frustrating, especially if the result was close. But trying to recover immediately can lead to faster and bigger losses.

Avoid thoughts like:

  • “I will recover everything in the next match.”
  • “One more round will fix it.”
  • “I already lost, so I should continue.”
  • “I cannot stop until I am back to zero.”

The safer response to a loss is to pause. Close the page, take a break, and return only when you are calm. If you have reached your budget, stop for the day.

Losses are part of gaming risk. Chasing them usually makes the situation worse.

Warning signs

Warning Signs of Problem Gambling

Gaming may be becoming harmful if it starts affecting your emotions, money, time, relationships, or responsibilities.

You should stop and seek help if you notice any of these signs:

  • You spend more money than planned
  • You hide gaming activity from family or friends
  • You borrow money to continue playing
  • You feel anxious, angry, or guilty after playing
  • You try to recover losses quickly
  • You miss work, study, sleep, or family time
  • You use bill money or emergency savings
  • You keep playing even when you want to stop
  • You argue with others about betting or casino activity
  • You feel that gaming is no longer fun but difficult to control
If even one or two of these signs feel familiar, take them seriously. Early action is easier than waiting until the problem grows.
